SCANNING AN ARTIFICIAL BRAIN

Deep learning is making big waves in the tech world, but the concept can be a little opaque. Neural nets are frequently compared to the human brain so I've taken the popular metaphor a little further by making an 'MRI scan' for an image classifier, specifically Google's Inception V3 architecture. When the network sees an image, different 'neurons' get excited and light up. The system guesses what's in the image by highlighting squares on a 32x32 grid and each square represents one of a thousand categories like 'cat' or 'airplane.'

Use the arrow keys on your keyboard to rotate / zoom in. Created using keras.js and three.js
