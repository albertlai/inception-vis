SCANNING AN ARTIFICIAL BRAIN

a look inside a neural network

As the name implies, neural networks are based on a simplified model of the brain. Brains are made of interconnected cells called neurons whereas neural nets are composed of millions of 'artificial' neurons - mathematical representations instead of the messy stuff in your head. These artificial neurons are arranged in interconnected layers, hence the 'deep' in deep learning.

An MRI scan detects activity in the brain when the subject is stimulated; you may see a different pattern of activity when the subject sees a scary spider vs a baby seal. When a neural network is shown an image, different regions of artificial neurons also become 'activated.' This visualization shows those excited regions for Google's Inception V3 network - kind of a fake MRI scan right in your browser!
